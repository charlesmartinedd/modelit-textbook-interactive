Social and Economic Systems

In 2020, toilet paper vanished from stores. It wasn't a supply problemâ€”it was psychology.

Humans are social animals. We look at what others are doing to decide what we should do. Usually, this is good (learning language). Sometimes, it creates a disaster.

The Tragedy of the Commons

Imagine a shared resource (like toilet paper at a store). If everyone takes what they need, there is enough for everyone. But if one person gets scared and takes too much, everyone else gets scared too.

Feedback Loops in Society

This is a Positive Feedback Loop (which, remember, doesn't mean "good").
Fear -> Buy More -> Empty Shelf -> More Fear -> Buy Even More.

Understanding this helps us stop it. During shortages, stores limit purchase quantities (a "System Constraint") to break the loop.
