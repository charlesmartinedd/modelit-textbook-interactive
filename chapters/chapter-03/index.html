Energy Flows Through Everything

You ate a salad for lunch. Where did that energy come from‚Äîand where did it really start?

Energy is the currency of the universe. It cannot be created or destroyed, only changed from one form to another. This is the First Law of Thermodynamics, but we can just call it "The No-Free-Lunch Rule."

The 10% Rule

Here is the harsh reality of nature: transfers are expensive. Every time energy moves from one thing to another (like a rabbit eating grass), most of it is lost as Heat.

Why Are There So Few Lions?

Have you ever wondered why forests have millions of plants, thousands of bugs, hundreds of deer, but only one or two bears?

Because of the energy loss you just saw. By the time energy gets to the top, there just isn't much left. A predator needs a huge base of support to exist.

This flow of energy connects the Sun (an Earth System) to Plants (Living Systems) to Humans (Social Systems). If the sun dims, or the plants die, the whole chain collapses.
